## 1. Purpose

This policy sets out how staff at BrightPath Skills Training may use AI tools in their work.
It establishes clear boundaries for safe, ethical, and accountable use of AI tools and ensures
that learner welfare, data protection, and organisational governance are maintained at all times.

The purpose of this policy is to:

- Enable staff to use AI tools productively and safely
- Protect learners, clients, and the organisation from the risks of irresponsible AI use
- Ensure accountability and human oversight at all stages of AI use
- Provide clear escalation pathways when staff are unsure

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all staff, contractors, volunteers, and associated persons who use AI tools
in connection with BrightPath Skills Training activities. It covers all AI-assisted tools including
large language models, AI writing assistants, AI-powered productivity tools, and AI image
generators used in the course of work.

## 3. Approved AI Use

The following uses of AI tools are approved for staff use, subject to the conditions in this policy:

- Drafting administrative correspondence, meeting notes, and routine communications
- Generating first-draft lesson outlines, scheme-of-work templates, and resource ideas
- Summarising publicly available information for research and planning purposes
- Proofreading and grammar checking of documents that do not contain personal data
- Generating anonymised examples and synthetic scenarios for training purposes
- Supporting staff professional development and reflective practice using anonymised examples only
- Creating draft policies and procedures for human review (without including personal data)

All approved use requires human review of AI-generated outputs before they are shared,
published, or acted upon. Staff are accountable for any AI-generated content they use or share.

## 4. Prohibited AI Use

The following uses are strictly prohibited:

- Entering identifiable learner data, learner records, or learner contact details into any AI tool
- Entering safeguarding case details, disclosures, welfare concerns, or vulnerable learner
  information into any AI tool
- Entering staff personal data, HR records, disciplinary information, or performance data
  into any AI tool
- Entering confidential client records, contractual information, or commercially sensitive data
  into any AI tool
- Using AI tools to make decisions about learner progress, attainment, or welfare without
  human review
- Using AI tools to respond to, record, or process safeguarding disclosures
- Using AI tools to generate official compliance assessments, audit reports, or regulatory submissions

## 5. Learner Personal Data

Staff must not enter any learner personal data into AI tools. Learner personal data includes:

- Names, dates of birth, contact details, or any other identifying information
- Attendance records, assessment grades, or progress reports
- Health, disability, or special educational needs information
- Information about family circumstances or safeguarding status
- Any data that could identify a learner directly or indirectly

Any AI-assisted work involving learners must use anonymised or synthetic examples only.
When in doubt, remove or replace all identifying details before using any text with an AI tool.

## 6. Safeguarding Case Details

AI tools must not be used to process, store, respond to, or record safeguarding information
of any kind. Staff must not enter any of the following into AI tools:

- Safeguarding disclosures or concerns raised by learners, parents, or carers
- Vulnerable learner information or welfare referrals
- Child protection records or case notes
- Information about contact with external agencies (police, social services, NHS)

All safeguarding matters must be escalated to the Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) through
the agreed escalation pathway. AI tools play no role in safeguarding decision-making.

## 7. Human Review

All AI-generated content must be reviewed by a member of staff before it is shared, published,
or acted upon. Human review means:

- Reading the full output carefully, not just skimming
- Verifying any factual claims against a reliable source
- Checking that the content is accurate and appropriate for its intended audience
- Removing any inaccurate, misleading, or inappropriate content before use

Staff are accountable for AI-generated outputs they share or use. Delegating a task to an AI
tool does not remove staff accountability for the result.

## 8. Accountability

Each member of staff is personally accountable for their use of AI tools in line with this policy.
Managers are responsible for ensuring their teams understand and comply with this policy.
The senior leadership team is responsible for maintaining, reviewing, and updating this policy
at least annually.

Suspected breaches of this policy must be reported through the standard incident reporting pathway.
Breaches will be investigated and may result in disciplinary action.

## 9. Escalation

Staff who are unsure whether a particular use of AI is permitted should not proceed until they have
sought guidance from their line manager or the designated AI governance lead.

Concerns about AI use should be raised promptly. It is always appropriate to pause and check rather
than proceeding with an uncertain use of AI.

Suspected policy breaches — including accidental entry of personal data or safeguarding information
into an AI tool — should be reported immediately through the incident reporting pathway so that
appropriate steps can be taken.
