# Build 8 Phase 5 Notes

## What Phase 5 adds

Phase 5 adds a deterministic training follow-up and support plan. It identifies which actions require support, how urgent that support is, the type and intensity of help needed, the most suitable delivery method, and the associated training delivery risk.

The phase also provides organisation, related-build, and owner-role workload summaries, plus a prioritised list with practical recommendations.

## Why training follow-up matters during implementation

One workshop rarely completes the people side of AI adoption. Staff may need workflow examples, quality-review practice, responsible-use refreshers, confidence support, or practical coaching as implementation moves from pilot to routine use.

Phase 5 turns a general training flag into a specific follow-up plan that can be assigned and reviewed.

## How urgency and intensity are classified

Training urgency uses the action due date. Required follow-up is urgent within seven days, due soon within fourteen days, and routine after that.

Support intensity combines the follow-up flag, status, priority, due pressure, and support type:

- High support applies to blocked training actions or Critical/High-priority training actions due within seven days.
- Moderate support applies to follow-up due within fourteen days or foundation, workflow-specific, or responsible-use support.
- Light support applies to other required follow-up.
- No support required applies when the training flag is not active.

## Connection to Builds 1, 4, 5, 6, and 7

- Build 1 workflow actions may need practical demonstrations before a new pilot begins.
- Build 4 provides the strongest training and confidence follow-up workload.
- Build 5 report actions may need quality-review practice for AI-assisted drafting.
- Build 6 governance actions may create responsible-use refresher needs.
- Build 7 evidence actions may need peer support so staff can record and review adoption evidence consistently.

## Consulting use

A consultant could use Phase 5 to plan targeted coaching sessions, demonstrations, office hours, and refreshers. Organisation and owner-role summaries show where support capacity is needed, while the prioritised table keeps urgent or high-risk training actions visible.
