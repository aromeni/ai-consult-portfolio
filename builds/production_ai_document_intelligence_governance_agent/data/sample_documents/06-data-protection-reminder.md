## 1. Introduction

This reminder summarises the key data protection obligations that apply when staff use AI tools at BrightPath Skills Training. It supplements the AI Acceptable Use Policy and should be read alongside the organisation's full Data Protection Policy.

UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018 apply to how we collect, store, use, and share personal data. These laws do not make an exception for AI tools. When you enter personal data into an AI tool, you are sharing it with a third-party system, which is a form of data processing.

---

## 2. What Is Personal Data?

Personal data is any information that can identify a living individual, directly or indirectly. This includes:

- Names, addresses, email addresses, and telephone numbers.
- Learner reference numbers, registration numbers, and employer codes where these can identify a person.
- Assessment results, attendance records, progress notes, and Individual Learning Plans (ILPs).
- Health information, disability details, or learning support needs.
- Photographs, video recordings, or voice recordings of identifiable individuals.
- Job titles combined with employer names where this identifies the individual.
- Any combination of information that, taken together, identifies a person.

**Special category data** receives additional protection under UK GDPR. This includes health data, data about disability, racial or ethnic origin, religious beliefs, political opinions, trade union membership, genetic or biometric data, sexual orientation, and criminal conviction data.

---

## 3. AI Tools and Data Protection

When you use a tool such as ChatGPT, your prompt — everything you type into the input box — is sent to servers operated by the tool provider. Depending on the tool's terms of service and settings, this data may be stored, used to improve the model, or accessed by provider staff.

BrightPath Skills Training does not have a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with consumer AI tool providers that covers personal data relating to learners or staff. This means:

- Entering personal data into consumer AI tools is not covered by appropriate legal safeguards.
- Doing so constitutes an unauthorised disclosure of personal data.
- This is a breach of UK GDPR and a breach of BrightPath's Data Protection Policy.

Enterprise versions of some AI tools (e.g. ChatGPT Enterprise with a signed DPA) may provide stronger data protection guarantees. Any use of an enterprise AI tool must be approved by the Data Protection Lead before staff begin using it.

---

## 4. What You Must Not Enter into AI Tools

The following must never be entered into any AI tool without explicit written approval from the Data Protection Lead:

- Learner names, learner IDs, email addresses, or contact details.
- ILP content, assessment marks, attendance records, or progress tracker data.
- Safeguarding referral information, disclosure notes, or welfare concern records.
- Staff personal data, employment contracts, payroll data, or disciplinary records.
- Client organisation data, signed contracts, commercially sensitive correspondence.
- Any data relating to health, disability, protected characteristics, or criminal history.

If you need to use AI for a task that involves the above categories, you must first anonymise or fictionalise the data so that no real individual can be identified. Contact the Data Protection Lead if you are unsure how to do this safely.

---

## 5. What a Data Breach Looks Like in This Context

Examples of data breaches involving AI tools that staff must report immediately:

- Pasting a learner's name and assessment results into ChatGPT to ask for progress commentary.
- Entering a safeguarding concern note into an AI tool to help write a referral.
- Copying a client's contract details into an AI tool to produce a summary.
- Uploading a staff attendance spreadsheet to an AI document analysis tool.
- Sharing learner photographs or video recordings with any AI system.

If you believe a data breach has occurred, report it to the Data Protection Lead immediately. Do not attempt to resolve it yourself, delete files, or contact the AI provider directly. Timely reporting is required under UK GDPR — a delay of more than 72 hours without good reason may constitute a further breach.

---

## 6. Lawful Basis and Transparency

BrightPath's processing of personal data is based on a combination of contractual necessity, legitimate interests, legal obligation, and, where required, consent. The organisation's Privacy Notice explains how personal data is used.

AI tools are not listed in BrightPath's current Privacy Notice as a data processor used in learner or staff data processing. Until this is changed through a proper Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) and appropriate legal documentation, personal data must not be shared with AI tools.

---

## 7. Your Responsibilities

As a member of staff, you are responsible for:

- Knowing what personal data you handle as part of your role.
- Using AI tools only in accordance with the AI Acceptable Use Policy and this reminder.
- Reporting any suspected data breach or near-miss to the Data Protection Lead promptly.
- Completing the required annual data protection training.
- Raising questions about AI and data protection with the Data Protection Lead before proceeding with any uncertain use case.
