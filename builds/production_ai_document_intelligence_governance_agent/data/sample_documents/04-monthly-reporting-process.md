## 1. Purpose

This document describes the monthly reporting process at BrightPath Skills Training, including the role that AI tools may play in drafting narrative sections. It clarifies what data staff may use with AI assistance and what data must remain protected.

Monthly reports are submitted to the Senior Leadership Team (SLT) and, where applicable, to external commissioners or funding bodies. They must be accurate, appropriately evidenced, and signed off by a designated manager before submission.

---

## 2. Report Structure

The standard monthly report includes the following sections:

1. **Headline performance summary** — overall learner starts, completions, and withdrawals in aggregate (no individual names).
2. **Curriculum area commentary** — narrative summary of progress, issues, and highlights by subject area.
3. **Quality and improvement actions** — update on outstanding actions from the quality improvement plan.
4. **Safeguarding and welfare summary** — number of welfare referrals in aggregate, actions taken, trends. (This section must not name individuals.)
5. **Staff and capacity update** — brief note on staffing changes, absence, or capacity pressures.
6. **Financial and resource note** — headline budget position, material resource requests.
7. **Risks and issues** — current risk register items and mitigating actions.
8. **Recommended actions for SLT** — clear, numbered list of decisions or approvals requested.

---

## 3. Data Sources

Reports are compiled from the following data sources:

- The Management Information System (MIS) — aggregate learner data only, no individual learner identifiers.
- The Quality Improvement Plan tracker — shared staff document.
- The Risk Register — managed by the Vice Principal (Quality).
- Finance summaries provided by the Finance Officer.
- Curriculum area updates submitted by each area lead.

Staff must not copy individual learner records, named case files, or identifiable welfare information into report drafts or AI prompts.

---

## 4. Where AI Assistance Is Permitted

AI tools may be used to support the following reporting tasks:

- Drafting narrative text for the curriculum area commentary based on anonymised bullet points provided by the area lead.
- Summarising quality improvement action updates into concise report language.
- Structuring the risks and issues section into a consistent format.
- Generating suggested wording for the recommended actions section, based on notes from the management team.
- Producing first drafts of the staff and capacity update from brief notes.

In all cases, the area lead or designated manager must review, verify, and approve the AI-drafted content before it is included in the final report.

---

## 5. Where AI Assistance Is Not Permitted

AI tools must not be used for:

- Processing or summarising individual learner data, named learner records, or cohort-level data that could identify individuals.
- Drafting the safeguarding and welfare summary section. This must be written by the Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) or their delegate, without AI tool involvement.
- Generating financial data, budget figures, or resource calculations. These must come directly from the Finance Officer.
- Interpreting or summarising legal, regulatory, or compliance information without independent expert verification.

---

## 6. Approval and Sign-Off

All monthly reports must be approved by a designated manager before submission. The approval process is:

1. Area leads submit their section updates to the report coordinator by the 5th of each month.
2. The report coordinator compiles the draft report by the 8th of each month.
3. The Vice Principal (Quality) reviews the full draft and returns comments by the 12th.
4. The Principal signs off the final report by the 15th.
5. The signed report is submitted to the SLT and relevant external stakeholders by the 16th.

No AI-generated content may be submitted without completing the approval chain above.

---

## 7. Storage and Retention

Approved monthly reports must be saved in the designated management reports folder on the shared drive. Reports are retained for a minimum of seven years in line with the organisation's data retention policy.

Draft versions, including any AI-generated drafts, must not be retained beyond the approval of the final version. Draft files should be deleted or archived in a clearly labelled "Drafts — Not Approved" subfolder.

---

## 8. Quality Standards

Monthly reports are subject to random quality audit by the Quality and Standards Team. Audits assess:

- Accuracy of data and narrative consistency.
- Appropriate use of evidence to support claims.
- Clarity, conciseness, and professional tone.
- Correct completion of all required sections.
- Compliance with this process guide and the AI Acceptable Use Policy.

Reports that are found to include unreviewed AI-generated content, inaccurate data, or missing sign-off will be returned for correction and the issue will be reported to the Principal.
