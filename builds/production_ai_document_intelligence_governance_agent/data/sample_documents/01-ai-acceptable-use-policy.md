## 1. Purpose

This policy sets out how staff at BrightPath Skills Training may use artificial intelligence (AI) tools, including large language models such as ChatGPT, in their day-to-day work. It applies to all staff, whether employed, contracted, or working on a sessional basis.

The purpose of this policy is to enable responsible AI adoption whilst protecting learners, clients, and the organisation from avoidable harm.

BrightPath Skills Training supports the use of AI tools to reduce administrative burden, improve the quality of written materials, and support staff with research and planning tasks. This support is conditional on staff following the principles and boundaries set out in this policy.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All AI tools used on BrightPath systems, including but not limited to ChatGPT, Microsoft Copilot, Google Gemini, and any similar generative AI product.
- All staff accessing AI tools on BrightPath devices or using BrightPath accounts.
- Use of personal AI tools where output is used in BrightPath work.

This policy does not apply to AI tools embedded within approved software platforms (such as grammar checkers in word processors), provided those tools do not receive sensitive data as input.

---

## 3. Approved Uses

Staff may use AI tools for the following purposes, subject to the conditions in Section 5:

- Drafting lesson plans and schemes of work using anonymised or synthetic content.
- Generating email templates and internal communications drafts.
- Creating administrative document templates, report frameworks, and checklist structures.
- Summarising publicly available information for staff development purposes.
- Generating ideas, outlines, and structural suggestions for learning materials.
- Producing draft reflective notes and CPD summaries from anonymised bullet points.

All AI-generated outputs must be reviewed, edited, and approved by a qualified member of staff before being used, shared, or acted upon.

---

## 4. Prohibited Uses

Staff must not use AI tools for the following purposes under any circumstances:

- Inputting, processing, or analysing learner names, learner reference numbers, assessment results, attendance data, or any other identifiable learner information.
- Processing safeguarding referrals, disclosures, concerns, or case notes in any AI tool.
- Using AI to make or recommend assessment grading decisions, pass/fail determinations, or grade appeals.
- Inputting staff personal data, disciplinary records, absence records, or performance review notes.
- Using AI to draft or inform complaints decisions, disciplinary outcomes, or funding eligibility determinations.
- Sharing confidential client or partner organisation information with any AI tool.
- Using AI output as a substitute for professional judgement, particularly in learner welfare, safeguarding, assessment, or compliance matters.

If a staff member is uncertain whether a use is permitted, they must seek guidance from their line manager before proceeding.

---

## 5. Conditions for Approved Use

Where AI use is approved, the following conditions apply:

- Staff must not enter real personal data into any AI tool. Synthetic, anonymised, or fictional examples must be used in all cases.
- Staff must review all AI-generated content before use. AI tools can produce inaccurate, outdated, or misleading information.
- Staff must not present AI-generated content as their own professional assessment without independent review and verification.
- AI-generated outputs must be edited to reflect BrightPath tone, accuracy standards, and learner-centred values before distribution.
- Staff should apply scepticism to all AI outputs, particularly those that reference regulations, statistics, case law, or specific policy positions.

---

## 6. Human Review Requirement

Human review is mandatory before any AI-generated content is shared externally or used in a decision-making process. This applies regardless of the quality or confidence of the AI output.

Human review means that a qualified staff member has read, assessed, and taken responsibility for the accuracy and appropriateness of the content. Approving AI output without review is a breach of this policy.

---

## 7. Data Protection

Use of AI tools may constitute data processing under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018. Staff must ensure they do not input personal data into AI tools unless the organisation has established a lawful basis and appropriate contractual protections with the tool provider.

In practice, this means: do not enter names, contact details, learner IDs, health information, or any other information that identifies a real individual into any AI tool.

---

## 8. Accountability and Escalation

Each team leader is responsible for ensuring their team understands and follows this policy. Suspected breaches must be reported to the Data Protection Lead and the line manager as soon as possible.

Breaches of this policy, particularly those involving learner data, safeguarding information, or confidential client data, will be treated as serious incidents and may result in disciplinary action.

---

## 9. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually or following a significant incident, change in regulation, or material change to the AI tools in use across the organisation.
