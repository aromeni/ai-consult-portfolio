## 1. Overview

This guide describes the approved workflow for using AI tools to support lesson planning at BrightPath Skills Training. It is designed to help teaching staff save time on administrative drafting whilst maintaining the quality, accuracy, and learner-centredness that BrightPath lessons require.

AI tools may be used to generate lesson plan drafts, suggested activity structures, and learner engagement prompts. They may not be used to finalise lesson plans without human review, and they must not incorporate real learner data or learner-identifiable information at any stage.

---

## 2. Step-by-Step Lesson Planning Workflow

### Step 1: Define the lesson context (without personal data)

Before opening any AI tool, prepare your lesson context in anonymised form:

- Subject or qualification unit (e.g. "Employability Skills — Interview Preparation")
- Target level (e.g. Level 2 Adult Learner)
- Session duration (e.g. 90 minutes)
- Approximate group profile in general terms only (e.g. "mixed prior attainment, some learners returning to education after a gap")
- Key learning objectives (what should learners know, do, or understand by the end?)
- Any constraints (e.g. limited IT access, mixed literacy levels, online delivery)

Do not include learner names, reference numbers, individual progress data, or assessment history. Use general descriptors only.

### Step 2: Generate a draft lesson plan using AI

Open your approved AI tool and enter a structured prompt. A good prompt structure is:

> "Write a 90-minute lesson plan for adult learners at Level 2 studying interview preparation. The group has mixed prior attainment and limited IT access. Include a starter activity, two main learning activities, an assessment for learning check, and a plenary. Use plain English. Include facilitator notes."

Review the output before making any further requests. If the structure is broadly right, proceed to step 3. If not, ask ChatGPT to revise specific sections before moving on.

### Step 3: Human review and curriculum alignment

Before saving or sharing the draft, the teaching staff member must:

- Check that learning objectives are achievable within the time given.
- Confirm that activities are appropriate for the learner group profile.
- Verify that any resources or references mentioned are accurate and available.
- Ensure the lesson aligns with the relevant qualification or curriculum framework.
- Remove or replace any generic content that does not reflect BrightPath's approach.
- Add any locally required fields (e.g. session reference, funding stream, venue).

This review is mandatory. Do not skip it because the AI output looks good. AI tools frequently produce plausible but subtly incorrect content.

### Step 4: Edit and personalise

The draft lesson plan produced by AI should be a starting point, not a finished product. Edit it to:

- Reflect your professional knowledge of the subject.
- Incorporate any differentiation strategies your learners need.
- Add your own examples, analogies, or context-specific scenarios.
- Remove language that is vague, generic, or out of place.

A lesson plan that is obviously AI-generated, without personal professional touch, does not meet BrightPath quality standards.

### Step 5: Quality approval (for new units or significant changes)

For lesson plans associated with new qualification units, substantial curriculum changes, or pilot programmes, the draft must be reviewed by the Curriculum Lead before use. The Curriculum Lead will confirm:

- Alignment with the relevant awarding organisation specification.
- Appropriate challenge level for the target cohort.
- Inclusion of required assessment moments.
- Compliance with any external quality monitoring requirements.

Approval must be recorded using the standard Lesson Plan Sign-off form on the staff intranet.

### Step 6: Save and version control

Approved lesson plans must be saved in the designated staff shared area, using the naming convention:

`[Subject]-[Level]-[Unit]-[Version]-[Date].docx`

Example: `EmployabilitySkills-L2-InterviewPrep-v1.2-2025-03.docx`

Previous versions must not be deleted — archive them in the version history subfolder.

## 3. Limitations of AI-Assisted Lesson Planning

AI tools are not aware of:

- Your specific learners or their individual needs.
- Current awarding organisation specifications (these change regularly).
- BrightPath's internal quality frameworks and teaching and learning standards.
- Any locally specific requirements from funding bodies or commissioners.

Staff are responsible for ensuring that all of these factors are reflected in the final approved lesson plan. AI output is a draft, not a finished professional product.

---

## 4. Record Keeping

Where AI tools have been used in the production of a lesson plan, this should be noted in the document footer or version notes. For example: "Initial draft produced with AI assistance. Reviewed and approved by [name] on [date]."

This record is required for quality monitoring purposes and ensures transparency about how learning materials are produced.
