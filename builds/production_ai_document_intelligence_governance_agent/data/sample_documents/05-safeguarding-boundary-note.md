## Critical Notice

AI tools must never be used in connection with safeguarding matters. This is an absolute boundary. There are no exceptions.

If you are unsure whether something is a safeguarding matter, treat it as one and escalate to the Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) without using any AI tool.

---

## 1. What This Notice Covers

This notice explains why AI tools are categorically excluded from safeguarding processes at BrightPath Skills Training, what staff must do instead, and who to contact.

Safeguarding includes, but is not limited to:

- Concerns about child or adult at risk welfare.
- Disclosures of abuse, neglect, exploitation, or domestic violence.
- Concerns about radicalisation or extremism (Prevent duty).
- Self-harm or mental health crisis disclosures.
- Online safety incidents involving vulnerable learners.
- Any situation where a staff member is uncertain about a learner's safety or wellbeing.

---

## 2. Why AI Must Not Be Used for Safeguarding

AI tools, including ChatGPT and similar products, present the following risks in a safeguarding context:

**Accuracy failures.** AI tools can produce plausible but incorrect information. In a safeguarding context, incorrect guidance could lead to a delayed or failed response to a learner in danger. This is unacceptable.

**Data protection.** Entering safeguarding information into an AI tool constitutes processing of special category data under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018. BrightPath has no data processing agreement with AI tool providers that covers safeguarding data. Inputting such data would be a serious data protection breach.

**Professional responsibility.** Safeguarding decisions require professional judgement, statutory knowledge, and accountability. AI tools cannot hold professional accountability. Decisions based on AI outputs in a safeguarding context would not be defensible to a regulator, an inspection body, or a court.

**Bias and harm.** AI tools reflect patterns in training data. In sensitive areas involving vulnerable individuals, demographics, or social circumstances, AI outputs can reinforce harmful stereotypes or produce inappropriate responses.

**Confidentiality.** Safeguarding information is strictly confidential and shared only on a need-to-know basis with named individuals. Entering it into an AI tool creates an uncontrolled disclosure.

---

## 3. What Staff Must Do Instead

If a safeguarding concern arises:

1. **Do not use any AI tool.** Close any AI tool you have open before addressing the concern.
2. **Follow the BrightPath Safeguarding and Child Protection Policy.** This policy is available on the staff intranet and in the staff handbook.
3. **Contact the Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) immediately.** The DSL is the first point of contact for all safeguarding concerns.
4. **Complete a concern form using the approved paper or secure digital form only.** Do not draft or store safeguarding concern notes in any AI tool.
5. **In an emergency involving immediate risk of harm, contact emergency services (999) first, then notify the DSL.**

Safeguarding is everyone's responsibility. Do not delay, do not pass the concern to someone else without recording it, and do not use AI tools at any point in the process.

---

## 4. Designated Safeguarding Lead Contact

The Designated Safeguarding Lead for BrightPath Skills Training is a named individual available during business hours. Contact details are displayed in all teaching spaces, on the staff intranet, and in the staff handbook.

In the absence of the DSL, the Deputy Designated Safeguarding Lead assumes full DSL responsibilities. Contact details for both are in the staff handbook.

Out of hours, if there is immediate risk of harm, contact emergency services (999) or the local authority emergency duty team.

---

## 5. Training and Awareness

All staff must complete safeguarding training appropriate to their role. This includes:

- Induction safeguarding awareness training (all new staff, within first week).
- Annual safeguarding refresher training (all staff).
- Level 3 DSL-specific training (Designated Safeguarding Lead and Deputy, renewed every two years).

Completion of AI tool training or this boundary note does not replace or substitute for the required safeguarding training.

---

## 6. Reporting Breaches

If any staff member believes that safeguarding information has been entered into an AI tool — whether by themselves or a colleague — they must report this to the DSL and the Data Protection Lead immediately. This will be treated as a data protection incident and a potential safeguarding failure and will be investigated accordingly.
