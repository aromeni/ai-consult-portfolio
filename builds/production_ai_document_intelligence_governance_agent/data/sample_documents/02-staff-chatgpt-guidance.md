## 1. Introduction

This guidance note explains how BrightPath Skills Training staff can get the most from ChatGPT and similar AI tools, whilst staying within the boundaries set by the AI Acceptable Use Policy. It is intended as a practical companion to the policy, not a replacement for it.

If you have not already read the AI Acceptable Use Policy, please do so before using any AI tool in your work.

---

## 2. What ChatGPT Is and Is Not

ChatGPT is a large language model trained on large volumes of text data. It can produce fluent, structured written content based on the instructions you give it. It is useful for drafting, summarising, suggesting structures, and generating ideas.

ChatGPT is not a knowledge database, a search engine, a source of fact, or a professional expert. It can produce plausible-sounding but incorrect information. It does not know the current date, your organisation's specific policies, your learners, or your local context.

You should treat ChatGPT as a capable but unreliable first-draft assistant, not as an authority.

## 3. Writing Effective Prompts

The quality of ChatGPT output depends heavily on the quality of the instructions you provide. Use the following principles:

**Be specific.** Vague prompts produce vague outputs. Instead of "write a lesson plan", use "write a 60-minute lesson plan for adult learners studying employability skills, focusing on interview preparation, using a mix of individual and pair activities."

**Provide context.** Tell ChatGPT who the audience is, what the purpose is, and what format you need. More context produces more relevant output.

**Use constraints.** If you want a specific word count, reading level, tone, or structure, state it in the prompt. For example: "Write in plain English suitable for a learner with a reading age of 14. Keep paragraphs to a maximum of four sentences."

**Give examples.** If you have a format or style you want to match, paste a brief example into the prompt. ChatGPT will often adapt its style accordingly.

**Iterate.** If the first output is not right, do not start again. Ask ChatGPT to revise: "Make this more concise", "Add a section on safeguarding considerations", "Rewrite this to be less formal."

## 4. What Not to Enter into ChatGPT

Never enter any of the following into ChatGPT:

- Learner names, learner reference numbers, or any information that could identify a specific learner.
- Attendance records, assessment results, progress reviews, or ILP content.
- Safeguarding referrals, case notes, disclosures, or welfare concerns.
- Staff personal information, HR records, or performance review notes.
- Client organisation names, contracts, or commercially sensitive information.
- Complaints, disciplinary matters, or legal correspondence.

If you need to illustrate a scenario for a prompt, use fictional names and invented details. For example: "A learner called Alex (fictional) has missed three sessions and is struggling with written work. Draft a supportive email."

## 5. Checking ChatGPT Output

Before using any ChatGPT output, check:

- **Accuracy.** Are any factual claims correct? If ChatGPT cites a regulation, statistic, or named source, verify it independently before repeating it.
- **Tone.** Is the language appropriate for your audience, your organisation, and the context?
- **Completeness.** Has it covered everything it should? Are there gaps or missing points?
- **Originality.** Has it produced generic content that could apply to any organisation? If so, adapt it to reflect BrightPath's specific approach and values.
- **Responsibility.** Would you be comfortable putting your name to this as a professional? If not, revise it further.

Never share, publish, or act on AI-generated content without completing this review.

---

## 6. When to Ask for Help

If you are unsure whether a particular use of ChatGPT is permitted, ask your line manager before proceeding. If you believe a breach of the AI Acceptable Use Policy has occurred — your own or a colleague's — report it to the Data Protection Lead promptly.

AI tools change quickly. If you discover a capability or limitation that is not covered by this guidance, let the Digital Learning Lead know.

---

## 7. Summary: Safe Use in Three Steps

1. **Before using ChatGPT:** Check that your intended use is permitted under the AI Acceptable Use Policy.
2. **When writing your prompt:** Use fictional or anonymised content only. Never enter real personal data.
3. **After receiving output:** Review, verify, edit, and take professional responsibility before using the content.
