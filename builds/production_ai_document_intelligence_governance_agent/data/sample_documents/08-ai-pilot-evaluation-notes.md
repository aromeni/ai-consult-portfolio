## 1. Pilot Overview

BrightPath Skills Training ran a structured eight-week AI pilot from January to March 2025. The pilot was designed to test whether AI tools could provide measurable productivity and quality benefits in selected administrative and curriculum support tasks, within the boundaries established by the AI Acceptable Use Policy.

Twelve staff members volunteered to participate across three teams: teaching staff (six participants), administrative staff (three participants), and curriculum leads (three participants). Participation was voluntary and all participants received a briefing session and a copy of the relevant policy documents before the pilot began.

This document records the evaluation findings, grouped by task category, and provides recommendations for the next phase of AI adoption at BrightPath.

---

## 2. Tasks Evaluated

The pilot evaluated AI assistance for the following tasks:

- Lesson plan drafting (teaching staff)
- Email and letter drafting (administrative staff)
- Monthly narrative report drafting (curriculum leads)
- CPD reflection note drafting (all participants)
- FAQ and knowledge article drafting (digital learning team)

AI was not tested on safeguarding, assessment, finance, learner records, or disciplinary processes. These exclusions were communicated clearly at the pilot briefing and no participant reported attempting any excluded task.

---

## 3. Findings by Task Category

### 3.1 Lesson Plan Drafting

**Usefulness rating:** High

All six teaching staff participants reported that AI assistance reduced the time required to produce a first-draft lesson plan. Estimates of time saved ranged from 25 minutes to 45 minutes per session plan. The most common positive feedback was that AI produced a useful structure quickly, which the teacher then adapted and professionalised.

Common adjustments required:
- AI-generated activities were sometimes too generic and needed localising.
- AI occasionally referenced resources that did not exist or were outdated.
- Differentiation strategies needed to be added by the teacher in most cases.

All participants confirmed they had completed the required human review before finalising lesson plans.

**Recommendation:** Approve expanded use for lesson plan drafting with the existing review requirements in place. Develop a set of BrightPath-specific prompt templates to improve first-draft quality.

---

### 3.2 Email and Letter Drafting

**Usefulness rating:** Moderate to High

Administrative staff found AI most useful for standard correspondence: enrolment confirmations, reminder notices, and internal announcement templates. Staff reported that AI saved significant time on boilerplate communications that previously had to be written from scratch each time.

Common adjustments required:
- Tone sometimes needed softening for learner-facing communications.
- BrightPath branding, specific contact details, and policy references had to be added manually.
- Formal legal notices (e.g. withdrawal letters) required careful independent review for regulatory accuracy.

**Recommendation:** Approve for standard administrative correspondence. Develop a library of approved BrightPath prompt templates. Formal legal or regulatory correspondence must continue to involve a manager review and, where applicable, legal advice.

---

### 3.3 Monthly Narrative Report Drafting

**Usefulness rating:** Moderate

Curriculum leads found AI useful for converting bullet-pointed area updates into polished narrative paragraphs. The primary benefit was speed and consistency of language across sections.

Challenges identified:
- AI occasionally introduced claims or interpretations not present in the original bullet points. Reviewers had to check carefully for invented detail.
- The tone of AI-drafted sections sometimes felt more formal than BrightPath's usual internal reporting style.
- AI had no awareness of ongoing institutional context (historical performance trends, known issues, Commissioner priorities) — these always had to be added manually.

**Recommendation:** Approve for use in converting anonymised bullet-point notes into narrative paragraphs, subject to close review. Produce a briefing note for curriculum leads on common errors to watch for.

---

### 3.4 CPD Reflection Note Drafting

**Usefulness rating:** Low to Moderate

Participants had mixed views on AI assistance for CPD reflections. Some found it helpful for structuring a first draft from bullet notes. Others felt that the AI output sounded generic and did not reflect their personal professional development experience.

There is a risk that relying on AI for CPD reflections reduces their value for genuine professional development. Staff may produce polished-sounding reflections that do not represent honest self-assessment.

**Recommendation:** Permit AI assistance for structure and formatting only. Discourage AI use for the substantive content of reflective writing. Include a note in the CPD policy that reflections must represent the staff member's genuine professional assessment, not an AI-generated summary.

---

### 3.5 FAQ and Knowledge Article Drafting

**Usefulness rating:** High

The digital learning team found AI highly useful for drafting FAQ content and internal knowledge articles for the staff intranet. AI produced clear, well-structured content quickly. The main review effort was checking factual accuracy and adding BrightPath-specific information.

**Recommendation:** Approve and expand. Consider running a structured knowledge article sprint using AI tools as the first-draft production mechanism, with a two-stage human review process.

---

## 4. Governance and Compliance Observations

No safeguarding incidents, data protection breaches, or policy violations were reported during the pilot. The Data Protection Lead conducted a mid-pilot check and found no evidence of personal data being entered into AI tools.

Two participants asked for clarification about edge cases during the pilot, both of which were resolved by referring to the AI Acceptable Use Policy. This suggests the policy is functional but that staff would benefit from additional practical examples.

---

## 5. Recommendations for Next Phase

1. Extend AI-assisted lesson planning and administrative correspondence to all teaching and administrative staff, with the existing approval conditions in place.
2. Develop a BrightPath prompt library covering the most common approved use cases.
3. Provide a follow-up briefing for all staff on the data protection and safeguarding boundaries, using practical case examples.
4. Commission a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) for any planned expansion of AI use that moves beyond the current approved scope.
5. Review this pilot evaluation at the next SLT away-day and agree the AI adoption roadmap for academic year 2025–26.

---

## 6. Known Limitations of This Evaluation

This evaluation is based on a small voluntary sample of twelve staff over eight weeks. The findings are indicative, not statistically representative of the full BrightPath workforce. The tasks evaluated represent a subset of the total administrative and curriculum workload.

A full-scale evaluation would require a larger participant group, controlled measurement of time savings, a quality comparison between AI-assisted and non-AI-assisted outputs, and longer-term tracking of whether AI adoption changes staff confidence and professional development patterns.
