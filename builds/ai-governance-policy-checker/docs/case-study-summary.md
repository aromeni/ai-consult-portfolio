# BrightPath Skills Training AI Governance Policy Review

**Case Study — AI Governance Policy Checker · Build 6**

*This is a synthetic case study. BrightPath Skills Training is a fictional demo organisation created for portfolio demonstration purposes. No real client data, learner data, safeguarding information, or personal data is used.*

---

## Client Context

BrightPath Skills Training is a synthetic small UK training provider. The organisation has approximately 45 staff and delivers accredited and non-accredited training programmes to adult learners across vocational and professional development areas.

Staff have begun using AI tools informally — for drafting training materials, generating lesson outlines, summarising learner feedback, and producing administrative communications. However, the organisation does not have a clear AI governance framework, and its existing policies were written before AI tools became widely adopted.

The organisation has six existing policies that touch on AI and data use:

- AI Acceptable Use Policy
- Data Protection and AI Use Guidance
- Safeguarding and AI Boundary Policy
- Staff AI Tool Use Procedure
- AI Output Review Checklist
- AI Incident and Escalation Procedure

---

## Challenge

An AI readiness review identified that while BrightPath has a reasonable starting point in its six policies, significant governance gaps remain. The organisation needed clearer policy guidance across:

- **Approved AI tools** — which tools are approved, on what conditions, and by whom
- **Data boundaries** — what learner and client data can and cannot be processed by AI tools
- **Safeguarding** — absolute boundaries preventing AI from being used in welfare or safeguarding decisions
- **Human review** — when and how AI outputs must be reviewed before use
- **Escalation** — procedures for reporting AI incidents, errors, and boundary breaches
- **Accuracy and hallucination** — controls to verify AI-generated content before use
- **Bias and fairness** — assessing AI tools for bias risk, particularly in learner assessment
- **Staff training** — building staff AI literacy and safe-use capability
- **Monitoring and improvement** — reviewing and improving AI governance over time

Without structured gap analysis, the organisation could not prioritise policy improvements or communicate governance status to its board, funders, or regulators.

---

## Solution

The AI Governance Policy Checker reviewed BrightPath's synthetic 6-policy pack against a 12-domain responsible AI governance framework.

**Step 1 — Coverage Review**
Each of the 12 governance domains was assessed against the policy pack using keyword-based evidence matching. Coverage levels were calculated: Strong, Partial, Weak, or Not covered. High-priority gaps were flagged.

**Step 2 — Gap Analysis**
Coverage results were converted into a prioritised gap analysis. Each gap was classified by severity (Critical / High / Medium / Low), with missing evidence statements, risk statements, and first-step action hints.

**Step 3 — Recommendations**
Each gap generated a policy improvement recommendation, including: recommended action type, target policy, suggested owner, wording direction, implementation steps, review questions, and success criteria.

**Step 4 — Governance Maturity**
Coverage scores, gap severities, and recommendation priorities were combined into a governance maturity score (0–100) and maturity level: Initial / Developing / Defined / Managed / Optimised.

**Step 5 — Governance Report**
A full client-facing Markdown governance review report was assembled, including an executive summary, coverage review, gap analysis, recommendations, maturity summary, recommended next steps, and responsible-use boundaries.

**Step 6 — Export**
The governance report was exported as Markdown and as a professional PDF, with analytics tables and chart visualisations embedded. The PDF included a cover page, governance analytics, six charts, the full report content, and a responsible-use section.

## Outputs Generated

- Policy pack overview (6 synthetic policies reviewed against 12 domains)
- Governance framework coverage check: domain-by-domain coverage scores and levels
- Prioritised gap analysis: severity, missing evidence, risk statements, action hints
- Policy improvement recommendations: wording directions, implementation steps, success criteria
- Governance maturity score and domain-level maturity breakdown
- Client-facing Markdown governance review report
- PDF governance report with cover page, analytics, and charts
- Markdown and PDF download exports

## Responsible-Use Controls Applied

- Synthetic/demo policy text only — no real BrightPath data used
- Deterministic keyword-based analysis — no LLM or AI API calls
- All outputs carry responsible-use disclaimers
- Human review requirement stated throughout
- Maturity score is indicative, not a compliance certification
- Recommended wording directions are starting points, not approved policy text
- Gaps and risk statements are consulting prompts, not legal or regulatory risk findings
- Coverage scores are keyword-frequency indicators, not legal adequacy assessments

## Consulting Value

This prototype demonstrates how an AI consultant could structure a governance policy review engagement:

1. Load the client's policy pack
2. Apply a responsible AI governance framework
3. Identify coverage gaps with severity and priority
4. Generate policy improvement recommendations with wording directions
5. Score governance maturity and identify blockers
6. Deliver a written governance review report

In a real engagement, the same workflow would apply to actual client policies, with:
- Qualified legal, safeguarding, and governance professionals reviewing outputs
- Appropriate data protection controls for real policy handling
- Responsible owners and governance approvals before any policy change
- Human review gates at every stage

The tool enables an AI consultant to move from a client conversation about "do we have an AI policy?" to a structured, deliverable-backed governance assessment — efficiently and consistently.

---

## Limitations

- Synthetic/demo data only — not based on real BrightPath policies
- Keyword-based analysis — does not understand policy intent, quality, or legal adequacy
- Coverage scores are indicative, not compliance ratings
- Gap severity classifications are based on keyword frequency, not legal risk analysis
- Maturity score is a relative guide, not an audit opinion or certification
- Wording directions require qualified review before any real-world policy change
- Prototype only — not production-deployed, not authenticated, not for regulated use

---

## Next-Step Recommendation

Before undertaking a real governance policy review engagement, an AI consultant should:

- Engage appropriate legal, safeguarding, HR, and data protection professionals to review outputs
- Obtain governance sign-off and responsible owner confirmation before any policy change
- Implement appropriate data protection controls for handling real client policy documents
- Confirm accountability structures with the client
- Carry out a full gap-to-action closure review with the relevant policy owners
