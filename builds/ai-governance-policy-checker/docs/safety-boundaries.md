# Safety Boundaries — AI Governance Policy Checker

**Build 6 · BrightPath ChatGPT Mastery Project**

---

## Data Boundaries

- Use synthetic/demo organisation data only
- Do not use real client policies
- Do not use real learner data
- Do not use safeguarding case information
- Do not use confidential client records
- Do not use staff HR data
- Do not use personal data
- Do not use regulated information
- Do not upload, import, or process real documents of any kind

---

## Advice Boundaries

This prototype must not be presented as:

- Legal advice
- Safeguarding advice
- HR advice
- Compliance advice
- Data-protection advice
- Financial advice
- Professional governance advice
- A compliance certification system
- An approved policy framework

---

## System Boundaries

- This is not a production system
- This prototype is not deployed to production infrastructure
- No authentication or access controls are in place
- No persistent storage is used
- No external AI API calls are made (no OpenAI, Claude, LangChain, LlamaIndex, or similar)
- No document upload is implemented
- No real-time data processing is performed

---

## Human Review Requirement

All outputs from this prototype must be reviewed by a qualified human before any real-world use.

Responsible owners and appropriate governance approvals are required before any policy guidance, governance assessment, or recommendations are acted upon in a real organisational context.

---

## Recommendation Engine Boundaries

The Recommendations page (Phase 4) generates deterministic policy improvement recommendations only.

- It does not provide legal, safeguarding, HR, compliance, data-protection, financial, medical, academic-integrity, or professional governance advice
- It is not a compliance certification system
- Suggested wording directions are indicative only — they are starting points for policy review, not approved policy text
- Suggested wording directions must not be treated as legally approved, compliant, or operationally effective without review by appropriate responsible owners
- Priority classifications are based on gap severity and keyword frequency scoring, not legal or regulatory risk assessments
- Suggested owners are role suggestions only — actual accountability must be confirmed by the organisation
- Target policy suggestions are indicative — the actual policy to be updated must be determined by the responsible owner
- Human review is required before any policy change is made

---

## Gap Analysis Boundaries

The Gap Analysis page (Phase 3) performs a deterministic analysis of coverage results only.

- It does not provide legal, compliance, safeguarding, HR, data-protection, financial, medical, academic-integrity, or professional governance advice
- It is not a compliance certification system
- Gap severity classifications are indicative only — they are derived from keyword frequency and domain priority weighting on synthetic text
- A "Critical gap" does not mean the organisation is non-compliant, in breach, or at legal risk
- A "No significant gap" finding does not mean the policy is adequate, legally sound, or operationally effective
- Risk statements are generic consulting-style prompts, not specific legal or regulatory risk assessments
- Action hints are first-step suggestions only, not professional recommendations
- Qualified human review is required before any gap finding is acted upon

---

## Governance Report Boundaries

The Governance Report page (Phase 6) assembles a Markdown governance report from all available Build 6 outputs.

- The governance report is not a legal opinion, compliance certification, safeguarding assessment, HR assessment, data-protection assessment, or professional governance judgement
- It is not an external audit, regulatory review, or accreditation assessment
- The executive summary, next steps, and recommendations are deterministic consulting prompts, not professional advice
- Suggested wording directions are not legally approved policy text — they require review by appropriate responsible owners before any policy change
- The report is generated from synthetic/demo policy text only and must not be adapted for real client policies without appropriate governance, approvals, and responsible owners
- Human review and responsible-owner sign-off remain required before any real-world use of the report

---

## Governance Maturity Boundaries

The Governance Maturity page (Phase 5) calculates a deterministic governance maturity score from coverage results, gap analysis, and policy recommendations.

- It does not provide legal, safeguarding, HR, compliance, data-protection, financial, medical, academic-integrity, or professional governance advice
- It is not a compliance certification, audit opinion, legal assessment, compliance rating, safeguarding assessment, or professional governance judgement
- Maturity levels (Initial / Developing / Defined / Managed / Optimised) are indicative score bands, not formal compliance ratings or regulatory assessments
- Domain maturity scores are derived from keyword frequency and gap/recommendation severity — not from legal or regulatory risk analysis
- Adoption-readiness guidance is a starting-point prompt for the consulting conversation, not a professional governance recommendation or sign-off
- Maturity blockers are based on gap severity and recommendation priority in synthetic demo text — they do not represent real regulatory or legal risk findings
- Improvement priorities are indicative consulting prompts only, not professional governance, legal, or safeguarding recommendations
- Qualified human review is required before any maturity finding is acted upon in a real organisational context

---

## Coverage Review Boundaries

The Policy Checker (Phase 2) performs a deterministic keyword-based review only.

- It does not provide legal, compliance, safeguarding, HR, data-protection, financial, medical, academic-integrity, or professional governance advice
- It is not a compliance certification system
- Coverage scores are indicative only — they are based on keyword frequency in synthetic text
- A high coverage score does not mean the policy is adequate, legally sound, or operationally effective
- A low coverage score does not mean the policy is non-compliant or inadequate
- Qualified human review is required before any coverage finding is acted upon

---

## Export Centre Boundaries

The Export Centre (Phase 7) generates Markdown and PDF governance report exports, deterministic analytics, and chart visualisations from all available Build 6 outputs.

- All exports are generated from synthetic/demo policy text only
- The PDF governance report is not a legal opinion, compliance certification, safeguarding assessment, HR assessment, data-protection assessment, or professional governance judgement
- Analytics outputs (completion status, coverage levels, gap severities, recommendation priorities, maturity levels, governance scores) are derived deterministically from keyword-based scoring in synthetic text — not from legal or regulatory analysis
- Chart visualisations represent the synthetic governance review findings only — they are not real compliance data, regulatory findings, or organisational assessments
- Exported PDFs must not be adapted for real client policies or presented as professional governance reports without appropriate governance, approvals, and responsible owners
- The PDF export does not imply that the governance review is complete, adequate, or fit for regulatory submission
- Human review and responsible-owner approval are required before any exported report is used in a real-world governance or compliance context

---

## Completion Review Boundaries

The Completion Review page (Phase 8) generates a build completion review, portfolio summary, and case study from Build 6 outputs and documentation file checks.

- The completion review is a portfolio documentation tool — it does not constitute a professional assessment, audit, or compliance report
- Phase completion status is based on the presence of session state outputs and documentation files, not independent quality verification
- Output completion percentage is a coverage indicator only — it does not validate the quality or correctness of the outputs
- Portfolio notes and case study are for portfolio demonstration and communication purposes only
- The case study describes a synthetic scenario — BrightPath Skills Training is a fictional demo organisation
- Completion review Markdown and portfolio notes Markdown must not be presented as real client deliverables without full review and appropriate attribution
- Human review is required before any real-world use of the portfolio notes or case study in client or business development contexts

This tool is a policy-review support prototype for portfolio demonstration.

It demonstrates AI consulting product thinking — structured audit inputs, deterministic analysis, governance framework application, and responsible-use boundaries — without processing real data or providing real advice.
