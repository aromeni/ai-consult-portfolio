# Build 9 — Commercial Positioning

## One-Sentence Offer

An end-to-end AI adoption consulting dashboard that helps small organisations move from readiness assessment to governed implementation, adoption evidence, and client-facing progress reporting.

---

## Ideal Customer

A small or mid-sized organisation (5–50 staff) that has started exploring AI tools but lacks a structured framework for adoption, governance, and evidence tracking. Common sectors: education and training providers, professional services, advice and community services, healthcare administration.

The ideal customer is not an enterprise with a dedicated data science team. They are a senior leader or operations manager who wants practical, low-risk AI adoption support from a consultant who can explain the methodology clearly and produce structured evidence of progress.

---

## Core Pain Points

1. **No clear starting point.** Organisations want to use AI but do not know where to begin or how to prioritise.
2. **No governance confidence.** Leaders worry about responsible use, data privacy, and staff readiness — and have no structured way to address these concerns.
3. **No evidence of progress.** After initial AI experimentation, there is no structured record of what was done, what worked, or what the next step should be.
4. **No client-facing story.** Consultants who do this work produce ad-hoc deliverables rather than a coherent consulting methodology they can reuse and sell.

---

## Practical Outcomes

For a client organisation, engagement with this methodology would produce:

- A structured readiness assessment with workflow priorities.
- A staff training plan and delivered workshop.
- A governance and responsible-use review with documented controls.
- ROI and adoption evidence for internal reporting.
- A delivery and implementation tracker showing progress against agreed actions.
- A capstone consulting report summarising the full journey.

For the consultant, it produces:

- A repeatable methodology that can be applied to any client in the same sector.
- A portfolio of structured evidence that demonstrates commercial thinking.
- A client-facing reporting tool that reduces post-engagement write-up time.

---

## Consulting Delivery Use Case

A consultant using Build 9 as a live tool would follow this sequence:

1. Conduct a readiness assessment using the Build 1 tool. Add findings as stage evidence for the client.
2. Review document handling and information access using the Build 2/3 tool. Document the evidence.
3. Design and deliver a staff training workshop using the Build 4 tool. Record completion and confidence outputs.
4. Generate an initial consulting report using the Build 5 tool. Share with the client as a mid-point summary.
5. Review governance readiness using the Build 6 tool. Document policy gaps and controls.
6. Track ROI and adoption outcomes using the Build 7 tool. Produce internal impact evidence.
7. Track delivery actions using the Build 8 tool. Confirm what was implemented and what remains.
8. Open Build 9. The capstone dashboard assembles the full consulting story automatically. Export the evidence pack and share the capstone report.

Total elapsed time for the consultant: one structured engagement of 3–6 months. Total elapsed time to produce the capstone report from the dashboard: under 5 minutes.

---

## Productisation Potential

Build 9 could be productised in three ways:

1. **Consultant toolkit.** Sell access to the dashboard as a subscription product for independent AI consultants. Each consultant inputs their own client data; the methodology is the product.

2. **Client-facing portal.** Build a secure, authenticated version where each client can log in and see their own journey, evidence, and report. The consultant manages the data input; the client sees the output.

3. **Audit and accreditation tool.** Package the governance and readiness sections as a standalone assessment product. Organisations complete the assessment and receive a readiness score and recommended actions. No consultant required for the first stage.
