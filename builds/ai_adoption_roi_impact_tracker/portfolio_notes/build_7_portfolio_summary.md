# Build 7 — Portfolio Summary

**AI Adoption ROI and Impact Tracker**

---

## Problem It Solves

Small and medium organisations that have piloted AI tools often struggle to answer a basic question: *is the adoption working?*

They may have anecdotal impressions from staff, but they rarely have a structured place to record adoption metrics, check whether risk signals are at acceptable levels, assess whether staff training is translating into confident use, or make explicit decisions about whether to continue, scale, or stop a workflow.

Build 7 provides a structured, lightweight tracking system that turns pilot adoption evidence into consultable data without requiring a data analyst, a CRM, or a cloud database.

---

## Target Users

- **AI adoption consultants** working with small and medium organisations in sectors such as education, healthcare, advice services, and community organisations.
- **Portfolio reviewers** looking for a demonstration of applied Python consulting tooling.
- **Learning and development leads** who want to see how training confidence data can be tracked and reviewed after an AI pilot.
- **Governance leads** who need structured post-pilot evidence for board, trustee, or regulatory review.

---

## Key Features

| Feature | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Synthetic adoption metrics | Twelve workflow-level records across three organisations and four related builds |
| ROI summary engine | Hours saved, efficiency gain, synthetic value-equivalent estimates |
| Workflow impact analysis | Impact status, bottleneck detection, prioritised action list |
| Training readiness review | Completion rates, confidence change, readiness bands, adoption readiness |
| Risk and quality review | Quality and risk concern levels, responsible adoption status, scaling permissions |
| Decision tracker | Seven-tier decision classification with reasons and next actions |
| Client follow-up report | Portfolio and organisation-level Markdown report with all evidence sections |
| Export centre | Markdown, CSV, JSON, PDF, and PNG exports from a single page |

---

## Consulting Use Cases

**Post-pilot review meeting**
A consultant has run a two-month AI pilot with a small charity. Before the review meeting, they run Build 7, generate the organisation-level follow-up report, and download the Markdown file. They use it as the agenda for the meeting, working through each section: ROI, workflow impact, training readiness, risk, and decisions.

**Multi-organisation portfolio review**
A consultant manages pilots with three organisations at once. The portfolio-level report gives a single view of all twelve workflows across all three organisations, showing which decisions are urgent and which are on track.

**Board or trustee report**
A governance lead needs to demonstrate to the board that the AI pilot has been reviewed responsibly. The Markdown follow-up report provides a structured, evidence-based record of what was assessed, what decisions were reached, and what the next actions are.

**Internal training demonstration**
A training designer wants to show how AI tools could be integrated into a learning and development audit workflow. Build 7 provides a complete working example of how adoption metrics, training confidence data, and responsible use signals can be combined into a structured review.

---

## Why Synthetic Data Was Used

Synthetic data allows Build 7 to demonstrate the full analytical pipeline without handling real client information, staff data, or regulated records. It removes legal and ethical risk from the portfolio demonstration while producing realistic enough outputs to show what the tool could do with real data.

The synthetic records are designed to cover a realistic range of scenarios:

- Workflows that are working well and ready to scale.
- Workflows with training gaps that need support before continuing.
- Workflows with quality or risk signals that require controls before expansion.
- Workflows that have been paused or stopped and need governance review.

## What It Demonstrates Commercially

Build 7 demonstrates practical Python consulting engineering skills applied to a real consulting problem. It shows:

- **Deterministic classification logic** — structured decision-making without LLM dependency.
- **Multi-engine analytical architecture** — five separate analytical engines combined into a coherent output.
- **Responsible-use orientation** — explicit attention to quality, risk, governance, and responsible scaling.
- **Client-facing output** — a Markdown report designed to be shared directly, without translation.
- **Export capability** — CSV, JSON, PDF, and PNG outputs from a single consistent data source.
- **Test-driven development** — all logic covered by pytest with deterministic fixtures.
- **British English consistency** — appropriate for UK public sector, charity, and education clients.

## How It Supports AI Adoption Consulting

A consultant who builds a version of this system for a real client can:

1. Record adoption metrics after each review period in a structured format.
2. Generate a follow-up report automatically without manual formatting.
3. Show clients a consistent view of how their AI pilots are performing across all workflows.
4. Make explicit governance-ready decisions about whether to continue, pause, or scale each workflow.
5. Produce a client-ready document that demonstrates responsible, evidence-based AI adoption practice.

Build 7 positions the consultant as someone who brings structure, evidence, and accountability to AI adoption — not just enthusiasm or general advice.
