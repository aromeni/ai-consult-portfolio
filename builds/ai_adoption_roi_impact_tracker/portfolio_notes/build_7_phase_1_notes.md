# Build 7 Phase 1 Notes

## What Phase 1 adds

Phase 1 creates the foundation for an AI Adoption ROI and Impact Tracker. It adds a Streamlit scaffold, synthetic organisation data, workflow-level adoption metrics, review decision examples, validation helpers, basic non-financial calculations, tests, and practical portfolio documentation.

The current phase deliberately avoids advanced ROI scoring, charts, report exports, recommendations, databases, authentication, file uploads, external AI APIs, and real-world data handling.

## Why synthetic adoption metrics matter

Synthetic adoption metrics let a consultant demonstrate the measurement workflow without exposing sensitive information. They make it possible to test the logic, show the client journey, and explain what evidence should be collected after an AI pilot.

This is useful because adoption value is not just about whether a tool exists. A responsible follow-up should ask whether staff confidence improved, whether workflows became faster, whether quality issues appeared, whether near misses were logged, and whether the organisation should stop, continue, review, or scale.

## Connection to Build 1

Build 1 identifies candidate workflows, readiness gaps, and adoption opportunities. Build 7 turns those findings into follow-up tracking records. A workflow that was flagged as promising in the audit can later be monitored for time saved, pilot status, quality issues, and adoption decision.

## Connection to Build 4

Build 4 creates staff training and workshop materials. Build 7 tracks whether training appears to support adoption by recording confidence before and after, training completion rate, staff group, and practical evidence notes.

## Connection to Build 5

Build 5 produces consulting reports. Build 7 creates follow-up evidence that can be used in a later report update, such as progress against pilot workflows, logged risks, quality issues, and the reasoning behind stop, continue, review, or scale decisions.

## Connection to Build 6

Build 6 checks governance policy coverage. Build 7 monitors whether governance controls are working during adoption by tracking quality issues, risk incidents, near misses, review decisions, and owner roles.

## What a consultant could use this for

A consultant could use this tracker during a 30, 60, or 90-day post-pilot review. It provides a structured way to discuss whether AI adoption is saving time, increasing confidence, surfacing risks, and producing enough evidence to justify the next decision.

It also helps keep the conversation balanced. Time saving can be reviewed alongside human review quality, governance concerns, incidents, and staff confidence, rather than being treated as the only success measure.

## What should come next in Phase 2

Phase 2 should add a basic ROI summary that remains safe and synthetic. It should calculate non-sensitive benefit indicators, make the assumptions visible, and avoid any claim that the tool is producing a formal financial assessment.

Useful next steps include:

- summarising time saved by organisation, workflow, and related build
- separating continue, review, stop, and scale workflows
- adding clear assumptions for later money-based ROI work
- preparing the data model for future charts and client follow-up reports
- keeping all examples synthetic and suitable for portfolio demonstration
